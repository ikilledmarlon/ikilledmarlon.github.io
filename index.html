Track A — The System
01
System

Bar Structure & Market Language

Bar types, formations, and context classification. Buy/sell zones and how to use them for entry structuring. Time-of-day significance, the scalping advantage, and how to study like a professional.

02
System

Breakouts & Price Structure

The breakout process — spikes, legs, and implied breakouts. Assessing breakout strength with relative factors. Counting legs, fractal structure, bull/bear structural units, and what happens when everything fails.

03
System

Market Behavior I — Control & Volatility

Event-response dynamics. Inter- vs intra-bar energy. The three types of market control. Cyclical volatility, regime shifts, and the principle of ambiguity — why the market resists clarity.

04
System

Market Behavior II — Inertia & Tests

Market tempo and its probabilities. The first-attempt principle and why second attempts matter. Tests, the two-test principle, H1/L1 and H2/L2 setups — implied vs actual.

05
System

Market Behavior III — Symmetry & Two Sides

Symmetry and proportion — possibilities, exceptions, and how to read disproportionate structure. The principle of two sides, market-maker probabilities, and reading both buy- and sell-side context.

06
System

Day Structure & Session Context

Predictable day structures and their probabilities. Dominant feature identification, behavior shifts, overnight/pre-market context, opening gaps, and the opening range as a decision framework.

07
System

Ranges, Wedges & Reversals

Trading range mechanics — legs, buy/sell zones, and nested patterns. Wedges, triangles, and expanding structures. Reversal signals, the three price point principle, and minor vs major reversals.

08
System

Traps, Failures & Scalp Execution

Traps as failed breakouts. FOF setups (trend resumption), point-of-clarity traps, and market-maker trades. Avoid 50/50 scalping, limit vs stop entries, trading against trapped traders, and adjusting style to market cycle and day structure.
